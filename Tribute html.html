APJ Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, also known as the "Missile Man of India," had a remarkable career that spanned several decades and included significant contributions to science, technology, and public service.

    Early Life and Education

  • 1931: Born on October 15, in Rameshwaram, Tamil Nadu, India.
  • 1954: Graduated from St. Joseph's College, Tamil Nadu.
  • 1960: Completed a degree in Aerospace Engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology(MIT).

    • Career in Space and Defence Research

  • 1960: Joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) as scientist
  • 1969: Transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). Became the project director for the Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) program.
  • 1980: Launched the Rohini satellite for India's first ballistic missile.

    Leadership in Missile Development

  • 1982: Returned to DRDO and initiated the Integrated Guided Missile Development program (IGMDP)
  • 1988: Launched India's first ballistic missile, Prithvi.
  • 1989: Launched India's first ballistic missile, Agni.

    • Key Roles and Achievements

  • 1992 - 1999: Served as the Principal Scientific Advisor to the Government of India.
  • 1998: Played a pivotal role in the Pokhran-2 nuclear tests, making India a nuclear-armed state.

    • Presidency

  • 2002: Elected as the 11th President of India
  • 2002-2007: Served as the President, widely known as the "People's President" for his approachability and dedication to inspiring young minds.

    • Post-Presidency

  • 2007-2015: After leaving the presidency, Kalam continued his engagement with academia and education.

    • Awards and Recognition

  • 1981: Padma Bhushan
  • 1990: Padma Vibhushan
  • 1997: Bharat Ratna
  • 2007: King Charles II Medal
  • 2011: IEEE Honorary Membership

    • Death

  • 2015: Passed away on July 27 while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong.
